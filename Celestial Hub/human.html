Human In Space

Humans first ventured into space with Yuri Gagarin's historic orbit on April 12, 1961. Since then, space exploration has seen numerous milestones, including moon landings, spacewalks, and long-duration missions aboard space stations. These achievements reflect humanity's pursuit of knowledge and exploration beyond Earth's boundaries.

Yuri Gagarin's Spaceflight

Yuri Gagarin became the first human to travel into space. The Soviet pilot and cosmonaut launched from what is now Kazakhstan in his Vostok 1 spacecraft.He orbited Earth once in 1 hour and 29 minutes, reaching a maximum altitude of 187 miles (301 km), and landed near the Russian city of Saratov.The entire flight lasted 108 minutes, with Vostok 1 circling Earth at a speed of 27,400 kilometers per hour.

First To Pilot A Spaceplane

Joseph A. Walker piloted the North American X-15 to altitudes above the internationally recognized boundary of space on July 19, 1963. The widely accepted boundary for space is the Kármán line, which is set at 100 kilometers (62 miles) above sea level.Robert M. White also flew the X-15 and achieved altitudes that were near or above the Kármán line, contributing to the early development of spaceplane technology.

First To Walk In Space

Alexei Leonov was the first person to conduct a spacewalk on March 18, 1965. The Soviet cosmonaut floated outside his Voskhod 2 spacecraft for 12 minutes and 9 seconds, tethered by a 16-foot cable.Leonov was part of the Soviet Union's competition with the US to reach the moon. He crawled through an airlock, a pair of doors that keep air pressure inside the ship, and floated out into space 310 miles (500km) above Earth.

First To Perform A Space Rendezvous

Gemini 6A, piloted by Wally Schirra and Tom Stafford, achieved the first manned space rendezvous with Gemini 7, which was already in orbit. The two spacecraft came within one foot (about 30 cm) of each other and maintained station-keeping for several hours. This achievement marked a significant milestone in space exploration and laid the groundwork for future rendezvous and docking maneuvers in space.

First To Rendezvous And Bock, Piloted

Neil Armstrong and David Scott successfully docked the Gemini 8 spacecraft with the Agena target vehicle. This was the first time two spacecraft had docked together in Earth's orbit.The Gemini 8 mission was the sixth crewed spaceflight in NASA's Gemini program. The mission launched on March 16, 1966. The Gemini docking mechanisms use simple mechanical elements to allow two orbiting vehicles to initially engage.

First To Orbit The Moon

Frank Borman, James Lovell, and William Anders launched on the Apollo 8 mission. The mission was the first to send a crewed spacecraft beyond low Earth orbit and the first to orbit the moon. The astronauts spent 20 hours orbiting the moon, taking photos of the far side of the moon and Earthrise. The mission also tested the spacecraft and crew's performance in lunar orbit and the navigation required for a journey to the moon.

Fastest Speed Traveled By Human

Apollo 10 was the fourth crewed mission in the Apollo program, and the second to orbit the moon. The mission took place from May 18-26, 1969.The Apollo 10 mission was a "dress rehearsal" for the first moon landing, which took place two months later. The mission tested all aspects of a crewed lunar landing, except for the landing itself.

First To Land On Moon

Apollo 11 was the first spaceflight to land humans on the moon. The mission launched on July 16, 1969, and landed on July 20, 1969. The crew consisted of Commander Neil Armstrong, Command Module Pilot Michael Collins, and Lunar Module Pilot Edwin “Buzz” Aldrin. The Apollo 11 lunar module, also known as the "lem", carried the astronauts from lunar orbit to the moon's surface

First Space Station

Salyut 1 was the world's first space station, launched into low Earth orbit by the Soviet Union on April 19, 1971. It was a predecessor to today's International Space Station (ISS) Salyut 1 was a 18,430 kg space station manufactured by S. P. Korolev Rocket and Space Corporation Energia. It was launched from Baikonur Cosmodrome Site 81.

First Space Shuttle Flight

STS-1, launched on April 12, 1981, ending a long hiatus in American space flight. The mission carried astronauts John Young and Robert Crippen into orbit and orbited the Earth 37 times over 54.5 hours. The Space Shuttle fleet flew 135 missions from 1981 to 2011, helping to construct the International Space Station. The shuttles also docked with Russia's Mir space station nine times and flew to the International Space Station 36 times.

Mir Space Station

The Mir Space Station was a modular space station that operated in low Earth orbit from 1986 to 2001. It was the first modular space station and was assembled in orbit from 1986 to 1996. The Mir Space Station was the longest lasting and most elaborate space station to date. It was designed to house more cosmonauts on longer stays than the Soviet Union's Salyut space station. The Mir complex weighed 285,900 pounds and contained 12,400 cubic feet of habitable volume.

International Space Station

The International Space Station (ISS) is a large, permanently crewed laboratory that orbits Earth. It's the largest modular space station in low Earth orbit. The ISS is a collaboration between five space agencies: NASA, Roscosmos, JAXA, ESA, and CSA. It was designed between 1984 and 1993, and construction elements were built in the US, Canada, Japan, and Europe.

First Space Tourist

Dennis Tito is considered the first space tourist. In April 2001, the American engineer and entrepreneur traveled to the International Space Station (ISS) for eight days on a Russian Soyuz spacecraft. Tito paid a reported $20 million for the trip. Tito's trip was a major milestone for space travel and tourism. Since then, more suborbital and orbital tourism opportunities have become available.

First Spacewalk

Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov performed the first spacewalk on March 18, 1965. The spacewalk lasted 12 minutes and 9 seconds, and Leonov floated 310 miles (500 km) above Earth. Leonov's spacewalk was part of the Voskhod 2 mission, and his fellow cosmonaut Pavel Belyayev remained inside the spacecraft to monitor his progress.
