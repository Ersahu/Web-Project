News

Space news includes successful satellite launches, advancements in space exploration technologies, and international collaborations for future missions. Companies like SpaceX and NASA have achieved milestones, contributing to the evolving space landscape. Ongoing efforts focus on lunar exploration, Mars missions, and satellite constellations, shaping the future of space exploration.

India Lands Successfully On The Moon

On August 23, 2023, the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) successfully landed its Chandrayaan-3 spacecraft on the moon. This made India the fourth country to land on the moon, and the first to land in the south pole region.

NASA's First Asteroid Sample Lands On Earth

NASA's OSIRIS-REx spacecraft successfully delivered samples of asteroid Bennu to Earth on September 24, 2023. The sample was collected in October 2020.

China Space Station Crew Completes Spacewalk

According to the China Manned Space Agency, the Shenzhou-17 crew completed their first spacewalk on December 21, 2023 at 9:35 PM (Beijing Time). The Shenzhou-16 crew completed their first spacewalk on July 20, 2023. The spacewalk lasted about eight hours.

Richard Branson's Virgin Galactic Completes First Nanned Mission To Space

On June 29, 2023, Virgin Galactic, a space tourism company founded by Richard Branson, successfully completed its first commercial spaceflight with four passengers on board.

Hydrogen Detected In Lunar Samples

In November 2023, researchers from the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory (NRL) announced that they had detected hydrogen in lunar samples. The hydrogen was found in samples of moon rocks brought back during the Apollo missions.

Curiosity Rover Finds New Evidence Of Ancient Mars Rivers

According to a study published in October 2023, NASA's Curiosity rover has found evidence of ancient rivers on Mars. The rover's data analysis suggests that many of the craters on Mars may have once been habitable rivers.

Aditya-L1 is India's First Mission To Study The Sun

Aditya-L1 is India's first mission to study the Sun. The satellite was launched on September 2, 2023, from the Satish Dhawan Space Center in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh. The Aditya-L1 mission will study the Sun's outer atmosphere from a distance of about 1.5 million kilometers from Earth.

Nasa's Webb Reveals New Features In Heart Of Milky Way

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope has captured a detailed image of the Sagittarius C region, which is near the Milky Way's core. The image reveals a dense star-forming area with many protostars and infrared-dark clouds.
